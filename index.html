Core Frameworks

This project analyzes three fundamental models of Human-Computer Interaction as described in Dix et al. (Ch. 12).

Norman's Stages of Action
A descriptive model that focuses on the psychology of the user. [cite_start]It is particularly useful for identifying the Gulf of Execution (the difficulty in figuring out how to use the device) and the Gulf of Evaluation (the difficulty in understanding if the device worked correctly)[cite: 20]. This model helps designers understand why errors occur, especially in safety-critical systems like medical devices.
GOMS Analysis
A predictive model that describes the procedural knowledge required by a user to perform a task. [cite_start]It stands for Goals (what the user wants to achieve), Operators (basic actions), Methods (sequences of operators), and Selection Rules (logic for choosing between methods)[cite: 20]. [cite_start]GOMS is best used to model the efficiency of expert users performing routine tasks[cite: 20].
KLM Predictions
The Keystroke-Level Model (KLM) is a simplified, low-level version of GOMS. [cite_start]It provides a quantitative prediction of execution time by breaking down a task into primitive physical operators (like K for Keystroke, P for Pointing, and H for Homing)[cite: 20]. [cite_start]It assumes error-free performance and is used to compare the speed of different interface designs[cite: 20].
