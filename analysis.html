Task: Setting a 10ml/hr Infusion Rate

Analyzing the interaction steps required for a nurse to set a dosage on a standard medical pump. This comparison highlights where GOMS predicts expert speed and where Norman’s Action Cycle identifies potential for life-threatening errors.

In-depth Comparison: Norman’s 7 Stages vs. GOMS Framework
Interaction Stage Norman’s Action Cycle (Psychology) GOMS Component (Execution) Safety Risk / Analysis
Initial State Goal: Intention to administer 10ml/hr. Goal: SET-INFUSION-RATE. Errors here are high-level clinical mistakes.
Bridge 1 Plan: Decide to use the "Rate" button followed by numeric keys. Method: Keypad-Entry Method. Gulf of Execution: Can the nurse find the "Rate" button?
Motor Action Specify & Execute: Pressing [Rate], [1], [0], [Enter]. Operators: Press-Key(Rate), Press-Key(1), Press-Key(0). GOMS predicts the exact physical time (KLM) for these actions.
Bridge 2 Perceive & Interpret: Seeing the 10ml/hr confirmation on the LCD. (Not Modeled in GOMS) Gulf of Evaluation: If the screen is dim, the nurse may miss a typo (e.g., 100ml/hr).
Final State Evaluate: Does the screen match the doctor's order? Selection Rule: If display is correct, start pump. Critical check to prevent overdose before hitting "Start".

Critical Analysis Summary

As discussed in Dix et al., Chapter 12, hierarchical models like GOMS are ideal for calculating the efficiency of repetitive tasks for expert users. However, medical devices must be evaluated using Norman's Cycle because the most dangerous errors occur during Interpretation and Evaluation—the stages GOMS assumes will happen perfectly.
